Burger Equation¶

John S Butler john.s.butler@tudublin.ie Course Notes Github¶

Overview¶

This notebook will implement the Lax-Freidrich numerical method on the the Burger Equation.

The Burger Differential Equation¶

Consider the one-dimensional non-linear Burger Equation:

(989)¶\[\begin{equation} \frac{\partial u}{\partial t} +u\frac{\partial u}{\partial x}=0,\end{equation}\]

with the initial conditions

(990)¶\[\begin{equation} u(x,0)=1-\cos(x), \ \ 0 \leq x \leq 2\pi. \end{equation}\]

and wrap around boundary conditions.

This notebook will implement the Lax-Friedrich method to appoximate the solution of the Burger Equation. The Lax-Fredrich method was designed by Peter Lax (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Lax) and Kurt Otto Friedrichs (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kurt_Otto_Friedrichs).

Discete Grid¶

The region \(\Omega\) is discretised into a uniform mesh \(\Omega_h\). In the space \(x\) direction into \(N\) steps giving a stepsize of

(991)¶\[\begin{equation} h=\frac{1-0}{N},\end{equation}\]

resulting in

(992)¶\[\begin{equation}x[i]=0+ih, \ \ \ i=0,1,...,N,\end{equation}\]

and into \(N_t\) steps in the time \(t\) direction giving a stepsize of

(993)¶\[\begin{equation} k=\frac{1-0}{N_t}\end{equation}\]

resulting in

(994)¶\[\begin{equation}t[i]=0+ik, \ \ \ k=0,...,K.\end{equation}\]

The Figure below shows the discrete grid points for \(N=10\) and \(Nt=100\), the known boundary conditions (green), initial conditions (blue) and the unknown values (red) of the Heat Equation.

N=10
Nt=10
h=2*np.pi/N
k=1/Nt
r=k/(h*h)
time_steps=10
time=np.arange(0,(time_steps+.5)*k,k)
x=np.arange(0,2*np.pi+h/2,h)


X, Y = np.meshgrid(x, time)

fig = plt.figure()

(995)¶\[\begin{equation} w[0,j]=1-\cos(x[j]), \ \ 0 \leq x[j] \leq \pi, \end{equation}\]

The Figure below plots values of \(w[0,j]\) for the inital (blue) conditions for \(t[0]=0.\)

w=np.zeros((time_steps+1,N+1))

ineg = np.zeros(N+1)
for i in range(0,N+1):
   ipos[i] = i+1
   ineg[i] = i-1

ipos[N] = 0
ineg[0] = N

Lax-Friedrichs Method¶

The Lax-Friedrichs Method of the Burger Equation is,

(996)¶\[\begin{equation} \frac{w_{ij+1}-\frac{w_{ij+1}+w_{ij-1}}{2}}{k}+aw_{ij}\big(\frac{w_{i+1j}-w_{i-1j}}{2h}\big)=0. \end{equation}\]

Rearranging the equation we get

(997)¶\[\begin{equation} w_{ij+1}=\frac{w_{ij+1}+w_{ij-1}}{2}-w_{ij}\frac{\lambda}{2} a(w_{i+1j}-w_{i-1j}), \end{equation}\]

for \(i=0,...10\) where \(\lambda=\frac{k}{h}\).

This gives the formula for the unknown term \(w_{ij+1}\) at the \((ij+1)\) mesh points in terms of \(x[i]\) along the jth time row.

lamba=k/h
for j in range(0,time_steps):
    for i in range (0,N+1):
        w[j+1,i]=(w[j,int(ipos[i])]+w[j,int(ineg[i])])/2+lamba*w[j,i]/2*(-(w[j,int(ipos[i])]-w[j,int(ineg[i])]))
        

fig = plt.figure(figsize=(12,6))

plt.subplot(121)
for j in range (1,time_steps+1):
